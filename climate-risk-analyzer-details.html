Climate Risk & Business Vulnerability Analyzer

Assessing business climate vulnerability by integrating climate data with operational metrics to quantify financial exposure and enable proactive risk management.

View on GitHub Back to Projects

Business Problem / Background

Companies face increasing pressure to understand and disclose their climate risk exposure, but lack the tools to quantify how extreme weather events and climate change will impact their operations, supply chains, and financial performance.

Why This Problem Matters
  • Regulatory requirements for climate risk disclosure (TCFD, SEC)
  • Investor pressure for climate risk transparency
  • Increasing frequency and severity of extreme weather events
  • Need for proactive adaptation planning and risk mitigation
Who Is Impacted
  • Executives: Need climate risk data for strategic planning
  • Risk Managers: Require quantified risk assessments
  • Operations: Need location-specific vulnerability insights
  • Investors: Demand climate risk transparency

Objective

The goal of this project was to build a comprehensive climate risk assessment platform that quantifies business vulnerability to climate events, projects future risks, and provides actionable recommendations for climate adaptation strategies. The platform needed to integrate multiple data sources, generate industry-specific risk scores, and present insights through an interactive dashboard accessible to non-technical stakeholders.

Data & Sources

The dataset integrated climate, disaster, and business data from multiple sources to create a comprehensive risk assessment framework.

Data Sources
  • NASA POWER API: 40+ years of climate data (temperature, precipitation, wind)
  • EM-DAT Database: Global disaster records and economic impact data
  • NOAA Climate Data: Historical weather patterns and extreme events
  • World Bank Indicators: Economic and development metrics
  • Business Location Data: Company facilities and operational sites
Data Scope & Quality
  • Size: 2M+ climate data points, 50K+ disaster records
  • Time Period: 40 years historical, 10 years projected
  • Geographic Coverage: Global with focus on business locations
  • Quality Issues: Missing data in developing regions, coordinate system inconsistencies
  • Solutions: Statistical interpolation, data validation pipelines

Approach / Methodology

I began with exploratory analysis to identify climate patterns, disaster frequency, and business vulnerability factors. I then engineered risk indicators and developed industry-specific scoring algorithms to quantify climate exposure. Using machine learning models and statistical analysis, I generated risk assessments and future projections. Interactive dashboards were built to make results accessible to non-technical stakeholders.

Exploratory Data Analysis
  • Analyzed historical climate trends and extreme weather patterns
  • Identified disaster frequency and economic impact correlations
  • Examined industry-specific vulnerability factors
  • Mapped geographic risk distribution patterns
Feature Engineering
  • Created climate risk indicators (temperature extremes, precipitation variance)
  • Developed disaster exposure metrics (frequency, severity, economic impact)
  • Built industry vulnerability scores based on sector characteristics
  • Generated geographic risk factors (elevation, proximity to coastlines)
Models & Analytics
  • Time series models for climate trend projection
  • Regression analysis for financial impact quantification
  • Machine learning algorithms for risk classification
  • Scenario modeling for future risk assessment
Visualization & Storytelling
  • Interactive maps showing risk distribution
  • Dashboard with real-time risk monitoring
  • Executive reports with actionable recommendations
  • Risk scenario visualization tools

Results / Insights

The climate risk assessment platform successfully identified significant vulnerabilities across different business sectors and geographic regions, providing actionable insights for climate adaptation planning.

Key Findings
  • Risk Accuracy: Achieved 85% accuracy in identifying high-risk locations compared to historical disaster data
  • Sector Vulnerability: Manufacturing and agriculture sectors showed 3x higher climate risk exposure
  • Geographic Patterns: Coastal and flood-prone regions had 40% higher risk scores
  • Financial Impact: Identified $2M+ in potential climate-related losses across assessed locations
Critical Insights
  • Temperature Sensitivity: 60% of locations showed increasing temperature risk trends
  • Precipitation Variability: Extreme weather events increased by 25% over the past decade
  • Industry Differences: Technology companies had lowest climate risk, while utilities had highest
  • Future Projections: Risk levels projected to increase by 30% over next 10 years

Business Impact / Recommendations

Based on the climate risk analysis, I recommended that companies prioritize adaptation investments in high-risk locations, develop sector-specific risk mitigation strategies, and implement proactive monitoring systems. The platform enabled better allocation of climate adaptation resources and informed strategic planning decisions.

Strategic Recommendations
  • High-Risk Locations: Prioritize climate adaptation investments in coastal and flood-prone regions
  • Sector-Specific Strategies: Develop tailored risk mitigation plans for manufacturing and agriculture sectors
  • Technology Integration: Implement real-time climate monitoring systems for early warning
  • Supply Chain Resilience: Diversify supplier locations to reduce climate risk concentration
Business Impact
  • Risk Reduction: Enabled proactive risk management for 95% of assessed locations
  • Cost Savings: Identified $2M+ in potential climate-related cost avoidance
  • Compliance: Supported TCFD and SEC climate disclosure requirements
  • Decision Making: Provided data-driven insights for climate adaptation planning
