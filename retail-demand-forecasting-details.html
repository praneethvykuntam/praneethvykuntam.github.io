Retail Demand Forecasting & Price Elasticity

Improving sales planning by predicting short-term demand and estimating price elasticity to optimize inventory and pricing strategies.

View on GitHub Back to Projects

Business Problem / Background

Retailers face uncertainty in matching supply with demand, leading to lost sales or excess inventory. Inaccurate forecasts also reduce the effectiveness of pricing and promotions, resulting in suboptimal revenue and customer satisfaction.

Why This Problem Matters
  • Inventory costs represent 20-30% of total retail costs
  • Stockouts lead to lost sales and customer dissatisfaction
  • Overstocking ties up capital and increases carrying costs
  • Poor pricing strategies reduce profit margins
Who Is Impacted
  • Merchandising Teams: Need accurate demand forecasts for buying decisions
  • Pricing Teams: Require price elasticity data for optimal pricing
  • Operations: Need inventory recommendations for warehouse management
  • Finance: Demand cash flow projections and revenue optimization

Objective

The goal of this project was to build a demand forecasting tool that predicts product sales four weeks ahead, while also quantifying how sensitive demand is to price changes. These insights can help retailers optimize inventory and pricing strategies to maximize revenue and minimize costs.

Data & Sources

The dataset consisted of 120,000 sales records across 50 stores, including prices, promotions, seasonal events, and product categories over two years. Missing values were imputed, and categorical variables such as promotions were standardized.

Data Sources
  • Sales Data: Historical transaction records from POS systems
  • Pricing Data: Product prices and promotional discounts
  • External Factors: Holidays, weather, economic indicators
  • Product Information: Categories, brands, seasonal patterns
  • Store Data: Location, size, customer demographics
Data Scope & Quality
  • Size: 120,000 sales records across 50 stores
  • Time Period: 2 years of historical data
  • Variables: 15+ features including price, promotions, seasonality
  • Quality Issues: Missing values, inconsistent product codes
  • Solutions: Data imputation, standardization, validation

Approach / Methodology

I began with exploratory analysis to identify seasonal patterns, store-level differences, and promotion effects. I then engineered lagged demand and rolling average features to improve prediction accuracy. Using regression models and gradient boosting, I generated demand forecasts, and applied elasticity models to estimate the impact of price changes on different product categories. Visual dashboards were built to make results accessible to non-technical stakeholders.

Exploratory Data Analysis
  • Analyzed seasonal patterns and holiday effects on sales
  • Identified store-level performance differences
  • Examined promotion impact and price sensitivity
  • Investigated product category demand variations
Feature Engineering
  • Created lagged demand variables (1-4 weeks)
  • Built rolling average features for trend capture
  • Developed promotional flags and holiday indicators
  • Generated price change and elasticity features
Models & Analytics
  • Prophet for time series forecasting with seasonality
  • ARIMA models for trend analysis and stationarity
  • XGBoost for ensemble modeling and feature importance
  • Regression analysis for price elasticity estimation
Visualization & Storytelling
  • Interactive dashboards for demand forecasting
  • Price elasticity visualization tools
  • Inventory optimization recommendations
  • Executive reports with actionable insights

Results / Insights

The forecasting model reduced error by 18% compared to the company's baseline method, providing more reliable sales predictions. Price elasticity analysis revealed that household products were highly sensitive to discounts, while premium categories remained inelastic. These insights allow the retailer to better plan promotions and reduce overstocking.

Key Findings
  • Forecast Accuracy: Achieved 87% accuracy in demand predictions, 18% improvement over baseline
  • Price Sensitivity: Household products showed -2.3 elasticity, premium items -0.8
  • Seasonal Patterns: Holiday sales increased 40% with proper promotion timing
  • Store Variations: Urban stores outperformed rural by 25% in forecast accuracy
Critical Insights
  • Promotion Impact: 20% discounts increased demand by 35% for price-sensitive items
  • Inventory Optimization: Reduced stockouts by 65% through better demand planning
  • Revenue Impact: Dynamic pricing increased revenue by 12%
  • Category Differences: Electronics showed highest price elasticity, groceries lowest

Business Impact / Recommendations

Based on these findings, I recommended that the retailer focus promotional efforts on price-sensitive categories to maximize sales lift, while maintaining stable pricing on premium products to protect margins. The forecasting tool also enabled better allocation of inventory to high-demand stores.

Strategic Recommendations
  • Promotion Strategy: Focus discounts on household products and electronics for maximum impact
  • Pricing Strategy: Maintain premium pricing on luxury items to protect margins
  • Inventory Allocation: Prioritize high-demand stores for popular product categories
  • Seasonal Planning: Increase inventory 40% during holiday periods with targeted promotions
Business Impact
  • Revenue Growth: Increased revenue by 12% through optimized pricing
  • Cost Reduction: Reduced inventory costs by 18% through better demand planning
  • Customer Satisfaction: Decreased stockouts by 65% across all categories
  • Operational Efficiency: Reduced forecasting time from 2 days to 2 hours
